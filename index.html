About Me

A brief introduction

Currently pursuing PhD in the Electronics and Telecommunication (ETCE) Engineering Department of Jadavpur University, Kolkata. I work in the Imaging, Vision and Pattern Recognition (IVPR) Group led by my guide and Head of Department - Dr. Ananda S. Chowdhury. Researching on developing better detection, classification, segmentation and prediction algorithms for various brain disorders using various imaging data (radiological, histopathological, etc.) and phenotype/genotype data.
Before delving into research I worked in Mercedes-Benz Research & Development India (MBRDI) involving myself in stand alone tool development with Qt C++ and python scripting for automation. During my Master's education I worked in on pulmonary artery-vein segmentation from 3D CTA images. Also developed a tool in Qt C++ for the same.
Being a passionate techy I try to learn new things, especially in the web development domain and have freelancing experience on developing and hosting a website for a canadian client. I was a Microsoft Student Partner during my undergrad years, which led me to gain experience in developing Windows phone 8 and Windows Store apps. I am interested in a challenging research career in Computer Vision that will continuously motivate me to learn and develop advanced solutions to contemporary problems.

Ongoing work

Brain Tumor classification

Currently working on developing a multi-modal classification approach for classifying brain tumor into glioblastoma, oligodendroglioma and astrocytoma from MRI and histopathology data.

Project 1

September 2019 - November 2020

Alzheimer's Disease Classification

Used 3D DTI scans to develop a decision fusion model that performed four-way simultaneous classification (AD, EMCI, LMCI, Normal) using a novel modulated rank averaging method.

Publication
A. De, A.S. Chowdhury: DTI based Alzheimer's Disease Classification with Rank Modulated Fusion of CNNs and Random Forest, Expert Syst. Appl. 169 (2021), 114338.
Reprint

Graduate Research Work

Master's Thesis

June 2017 – May 2018

Segmentation of Conjoint Structures in Pulmonary CTA

Developed stand-alone tool (in Qt C++) to segment arteries and veins from 3D pulmonary vasculature using multi-scale opening of shortest paths generated using a few manually given artery-vein seed points.

Publication
De A., Das N., Sarkar R., Saha P.K., Basu S. (2019) Design of Synthetic 3-D Pulmonary Phantoms Using 2-D Graphical User Interface. In: Mandal J., Mukhopadhyay S., Dutta P., Dasgupta K.
